01 The Brand

Grown by nature, made by people.

UBalance Naturals makes high-potency liquid Ayurvedic formulations from real botanicals. The Tactile system puts the human hand back on the shelf — warm paper, two honest inks, the small imperfections of something actually printed. Craft you can almost feel through the screen.

02 Logo

The mark stays. It gets printed.

We keep the existing medallion wordmark — here it sits in green ink on warm stock, or knocked out to paper on a green field. The texture and grain live around it; the glyph itself stays clean so it survives the press at any size.

Primary — green ink on stock brightness(0)
Knockout — paper on green invert(1)
xx

Clear space & minimum size. Maintain padding equal to the medallion height (x) on every side. Keep grain and halftone clear of the mark itself. Never set the wordmark below 26px tall.

✓

Print it flat. One ink — green or knocked-out paper. The mark is always a solid, clean shape.

✓

Texture around, not on. Grain and halftone belong to the field, never inside the glyph.

✕

Don't misregister the mark, add gloss, gradient, or a hard drop shadow to the wordmark itself.

✕

Don't overprint the logo with the marigold ink; the mark stays a single solid colour.

03 Color

Two inks. One overprint.

The whole palette is two riso inks on warm paper. Forest green is the spine; marigold is the partner. Where they overlap they multiply into a deep olive — the third colour is a happy accident of printing, never mixed digitally. Fills carry grain and halftone; nothing is flat screen-colour.

04 Typography

Ink that soaks in.

Erode — a premium weathered serif whose contours look like ink bleeding into paper — leads every headline, with its italic carrying accent words over a marigold swipe. Synonym, a warm humanist grotesque, handles body, labels and UI. The eroded edges are the point: type that looks printed, not rendered.

05 Buttons & CTA

Stamped pills that press in.

Controls are full pills with a 1.5px ink outline and a hard offset shadow — like a rubber stamp lifted off the page. Green is the default action; marigold is the single loud moment. On press they sink into the paper. No soft glow, no bounce.

06 Components

Everything's a printed sticker.

Cards, chips and badges are die-cut stickers — rounded, ink-outlined, lifted off the page with a hard offset shadow. The halftone divider and overprint dots carry the print language through. Depth is a printed offset, never a soft blur.

07 Iconography

2px stroke. Rounded & friendly.

A single line-icon set — a chunky 2px stroke, round caps and joins on a 24px grid, sitting in die-cut sticker cells. Icons inherit currentColor and flip to paper on green or ink on marigold. Ornaments are the asterisk and the dot. No emoji.

08 Photography

Two-ink, halftone treatment.

Photography is pushed toward the print: lightly desaturated, multiplied onto warm paper, with a faint green ink-wash so it reads as a riso scan rather than a glossy product shot. Real botanicals, plainly arranged. Grain ties it to the page; text never sits on the image.

Treatment

Desaturated and multiplied onto warm paper with a faint green wash. Reads as a two-ink riso scan, not a glossy render.

Crop

Die-cut rectangle, 18px radius, 1.5px ink outline and a hard offset — a photo printed as a sticker.

Composition

Real botanicals plainly arranged on paper. Grain over the whole frame keeps it on the press.

09 Voice & Tone

Warm, plain, made by us.

Speak like the person who actually made it — friendly, specific, unpolished in the good way. Short lines, real numbers, a little warmth. The handmade system extends to language: no corporate gloss, no hype, no exclamation pile-ups, no emoji.

We write

"We make it in small batches. 3,825 mg of actives, the same recipe every time."

Warm and human. First-person, specific amounts, the maker in the sentence. Honest about what it is and isn't.

We avoid

"The ultimate game-changing miracle boost!!!"

No hype, no exclamation, no corporate polish, no emoji. If it sounds like an ad and not a person, rewrite it.
