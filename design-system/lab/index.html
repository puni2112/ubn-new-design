01 The Brand

We don't make claims. We publish readings.

UBalance Naturals makes high-potency liquid Ayurvedic formulations. The Lab system treats every product as an instrument output — dose, assay, lot and result, stated in figures. Forest green is no longer a leaf; it is a verified value.

P.01

Precise

Every figure is exact and sourced — 3,825 mg, not "high potency". Tabular numerals, real units, no rounding for effect.

P.02

Verifiable

Each batch carries a certificate of analysis. The design surfaces the proof rather than asking for trust.

P.03

Disciplined

A grid of hairlines, no ornament, no glow. Restraint is the signal of a serious formulation.

02 Logo

The mark stays. The method changes.

We keep the existing medallion wordmark — here it sits in deep forest ink, registered to the grid. No gold, no recolor. The mark is placed with crosshair precision and surrounded by measured clear space.

03 Color

Instrument greys. One verified green.

The palette is cool and clinical — instrument grey-green, pure-white panels, hairline rules. A single clinical green is the only saturated colour, reserved for verified data, the active state and the figure that passed. Anywhere else, it stops meaning "proven".

07 Iconography

08 Photography

Specimen light. Documented, not styled.

Product shot as specimen — even, shadowless light on a neutral ground, slightly desaturated, captioned with a mono data tag. The botanical is evidence, not decoration. Text never sits on the image; the caption sits beside it.

Treatment

Even, shadowless daylight; slightly desaturated. Neutral grey or white ground. Never moody, never warm-filtered.

Crop

Clean rectangle, 3px radius. A mono data caption sits inside the lower-left corner.

Composition

Bottle centred as specimen with the actives documented around it. Generous, gridded negative space.

09 Voice & Tone

State the figure. Cite the method.

Speak like a report. Give the number, name the test, stop. The clinical system extends to language — no hype, no exclamation, no adjectives doing a verb's job. Confidence reads as a citation.

We write

"3,825 mg of actives per 10ml dose. 99.2% assay, HPLC-verified, every lot."

Figures first, units always, method named. One claim per line. Honest about what the data does and doesn't show.

We avoid

"The ultimate game-changing miracle boost!!!"

No hype, no exclamation, no vague superlatives, no emoji. If a word isn't a measurement or a fact, question whether it belongs.
