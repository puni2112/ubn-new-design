02 Logo

The mark stays. The habitat changes.

We keep the existing medallion wordmark — here it lives in forest ink. No gold, no recolor. Deep green on natural paper, reversed on forest, surrounded by generous clear space.

Primary — forest ink on paper filter: brightness(0)
Reversed — on forest green brightness(0) invert(1)
xx

Clear space & minimum size. Maintain padding equal to the medallion height (x) on every side. Never set the wordmark below 26px tall. The mark always scales as one locked unit.

One ink only. Use forest green, paper white or full reverse. The mark carries no accent color in this system.

Lock proportions. Scale uniformly; medallion and wordmark always move together.

Don't recolor, add shadow, gradient, rotate, or reintroduce the old gold treatment.

Don't place on a busy photo without a solid forest or paper field behind it.

07 Iconography

Duotone. Warmer, not flat.

A clean line set on its own can read flat next to a constant grotesque. So the set gains a duotone treatment — a soft leaf-tint fill behind a 1.6px stroke, on a 24px grid. The fill carries warmth and mood; the stroke keeps it crisp and scalable. A broader vocabulary covers botanical, vitality and ritual.

08 Photography

Product among real botanicals — root, leaf and stone — in warm natural light, with room to breathe. Imagery stays in full, living color: green and earthy, never desaturated. Soft-edged crops with a generous radius; text never sits on the image.

09 Voice & Tone

Fewer words. More certainty.

Speak in short, declarative lines. State the fact, cite the number, stop. The natural system extends to language — no hype, no exclamation, no filler. Confidence reads as economy — and weight, not volume, does the emphasis.

We write

Not a sudden rush. A steady rise in performance.

Short fragments. One claim per line. Specific numbers (3,825 mg · 247 reviews) carry the weight. Honest about what it does and doesn't do.

We avoid

The ultimate game-changing miracle boost!!!

No hype, no exclamation pile-ups, no vague superlatives, no emoji. If a word can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
